Reflections on Freshman Year of College

6 Jun 2019
Kevin Lutz

I have been out of school now for about 3 weeks and I figured now is a good time to recap my first year college experience. So far, college has been the best time of my life and I am so excited for it to continue for the next three years. Over the past 9 months I have met so many incredible people and had so many great times. I've seen myself grow into a person I never thought I would be; more outgoing, excited to meet new people, eager to go out and do things, and overall just happier. I have always been a quiet and reserved person, and to a great extent I still am, but so much has changed in such a short period of time, and in a very good way. I believe it is this social growth that gave me the drive and courage to go and get my first job, which I am absolutely loving as well! I have become more spontaneous and adventurous. More open to try new things and talk to people. I have learned to own who I am and show it to the world.

I didn't only learn social skills in college, of course. I have learned so much about Java to where I am comfortable calling it one of my main languages. College has also forced me to learn how to take good notes and study. Never throughout my entire academic career leading up to college had I ever studied for anything, not even the SAT. It was when finals week rolled around during my first semester when I realized how much I didn't remember the material for my Ancient Philosophy class. For the first time, I had to study. It all paid off in the end however, in my first semester I got straight A's and ended up with a 3.88 GPA, landing myself on the highest honor Dean's list. That success continued into my second semester (as long as we don't count Calculus), and my overall cumulative GPA is now a 3.53. I hope to continue this trend of academic growth and success into the next 3 years at Pace.

All in all, college has been a period of extreme personal growth for me and I'm only a quarter of the way through. I am so excited to see what else college has in store for me, as I'm sure it will be awesome.


5 Technology Trends To Keep An Eye On in 2019

2 Feb 2019
Kevin Lutz

This article discusses some of the most popular things in tech to look forward to and keep an eye on for 2019.

1. Truly Bezel-less Smartphones

2018 was the year of the "notch". That horrible eyesore that houses front-facing camera and speaker equipment in most flagship smartphones released during the year. I hope and believe that companies will find ways to clean up the "foreheads" and "chins" of smartphones in 2019. Some beacons of hope include mechanical implementations such as in the Oppo X and the hole-punch idea shown by Samsung.

2. Self-Driving and (more) Electric Cars

We saw some big name, longstanding automotive companies add some electric cars to their lineups. I predict that we will see these a lot more of this in 2019. Along with cars becoming electric, we should see greater advancements in self-driving cars as more companies compete with Tesla in this space. This is big news for the future of taxis, long distance shipping hauls, etc. One potential benefit could be reduced prices for express shipping from large companies that can afford to acquire a fleet of self-driving electric cars.

3. Foldable Smartphones

This one I believe is the most bold of the predictions on this list. Mainly because moving pieces = less reliability. However, it is interesting to think that we may soon be able to have a device that can effectively function as both a tablet and a pocket-sized phone. The early demonstrations shown at CES are not very promising, with odd screen glitches occurring during transition and a whole lot of bulk. The glitches are to be expected with early proof of concept devices, but people won't buy a phone that is an inch thick when folded after years of trying to slim down our devices.

4. Quantum Computing

Although it is still in it's infancy and certainly isn't tailored to consumers, quantum computing should make some significant progress in 2019. IBM is the forerunner of the quantum computing realm with their IBM Q. To me, the most interesting thing about IBM Q is that anyone can tinker with it. Without a doubt, opening up a technology to everyone is the best way to move it forward. There is a steep learning curve, but you can try your hand at quantum logic on IBM's Q Experience.

5. Security Breaches followed by Improved Cyber Security

This prediction is already showing itself with Apple's group FaceTime bug. Security breaches, exploits, and information leaks are never good. Unfortunately, expect to see a few big ones in 2019. The cat and mouse game between hackers and cyber security professionals gets crazier and more serious each year, and 2019 will be no different. The good thing about these things happening is that it makes things more secure for the future. Just as hackers come up with new ways to exploit, companies come up with better ways to stop them. Let's just hope that most of these events in 2019 are harmless.
